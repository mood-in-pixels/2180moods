Targeted Mood Tips



Yoga

Go to yoga class every week

Done right, yoga can give you a resistance workout, more flexibility, and stress-busting skills. If you're in a fast­paced class you might even get some cardio in.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Content
  • Happy
  • Stressed
Learn more

Volunteer

Give back once a week

Helping others can benefit you more than you think. Regular volunteering can boost self-esteem, help you meet new people, and even reduce your risk of depression.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Angry
  • Compassionate
  • Depressed
Learn more

Gratitude

Be grateful

When things aren't going your way, it's easy to lose sight of the good stuff. Think of one thing you're grateful for each day. It'll improve you're optimistism and stress management.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Guilty
  • Compassionate
  • Stressed
Learn more

Sleep

Get 7 to 8 hours of sleep a night

Most adults need around 7 or 8 hours of solid sleep a night to feel good, focus on work, and stay healthy. If you need more sleep, try to get to bed earlier, and don't exercise or drink caffeine or eat big meals before bed.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Focus
  • Stressed
  • Energetic
Learn more

Write

Jot something down every day

Whether it's a list to help managedaily stressors, a simple record of your day, a way to work out your feelings, or a creative outlet, journaling can help you better understand yourself, your body, and your place in the world.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Compassionate
  • Content
  • Depressed
Learn more

Meditate

Meditate once a day

Find your bliss, whether it's meditation, prayer, or art ... that mental calm and focus can help your body too, reducing stress hormones and even lowering blood pressure. Adhereing to daily practice optimizes these benefits.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Stressed
  • Content
  • Focus
Learn more

Eat well

Eat a balanced diet, right for you

Investing time and energy into understanding what your body needs is essential to our health and well-being. Honor your body's nutritional needs and aim for a healthy, balanced diet.

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Energetic
  • Stressed
  • Content
Learn more

Work out

Exercise regularly

Starting a fitness routine between the ages of 20 and 40 that includes three sweat sessions a week could reduce one's risk of depression by about 16 percent. (JAMA, 2014)

  • Targets the following moods...
  • Depressed
  • Happy
  • Energy
Learn more
